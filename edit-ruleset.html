Weapons
Roll formulas for weapon attacks and damage. These use the standard formula engine and can reference stats (DEX, STR), stat mods (DEXMOD, STRMOD), primary skills by name (Melee, Ranged), and weapon constants: DMG (weapon damage dice), PEN (armor pierced), ATK (attack result — 0 at display time, replaced when chained from an attack roll), and DMGMOD (weapon's own dmg bonus, ranged only).
Split rule: any variable ending in MOD is added as a flat bonus to the roll result; everything else contributes to the D10 dice pool. So DEX + Melee + DEXMOD means roll DEX + Melee dice, then add DEXMOD successes at the end.
Weapon Tags
Short descriptive labels players can assign to individual weapons (e.g. Silenced, Two-Handed, Bleeding). Tags don't auto-apply mechanics — they're informational for now and act as lookup keys for later QoL features. Describe each tag here so players know what it does when they see it on a weapon card.
Tag Categories
Optional nested groups for organizing tags. Uncategorized tags go under the built-in "Uncategorized" group by default. Click a header in the tag list below to collapse the whole group.
Categories
Organize your items into a category tree. Nest categories as deep as you want — e.g. Weapons → Firearms → Pistols. Items without a category fall into Miscellaneous automatically. The tree mirrors what players see when browsing the catalog on their character sheet.

Dimension Presets
Named size templates players can pick when creating items instead of typing L×W×H every time. A preset can also carry a default weight. Edit or delete the shipped built-ins if your setting uses different conventions; add custom presets for shapes unique to your ruleset. Picking a preset on an item copies its values — the item stays independent of the preset afterwards.
Items
Everything carryable: weapons, tools, armor, consumables, containers. An item becomes a container when its Also a container toggle is on — ammo pouches, backpacks, and weapon holsters all live here as items with a capacity block. Dimensions and weight feed the packing math on the character sheet. Packing Efficiency (on containers): 1.0 = perfectly-fitted hard case, 0.75 = typical soft bag, 0.5 = loose sack or odd shape.
Ability Catalogue

The Catalogue holds every Ability, Artifact, Artifact Assembly, and Consumable Assembly your Players can choose from. It has its own editor — Categories, Builders, Parameters, Features, Flaws, system text, the works.
